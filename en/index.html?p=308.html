Preliminary Questionnaire for the application to the Round 2

Welcome to the submission form for the Open Contracting Innovation Challenge. The minimum requirement at the start of the challenge is that you have an idea for a procurement data-based tool that can help to deliver significant, quantifiable progress between May to December  2021.

 

During the first incubation phase, we will help teams to structure their idea and prepare presentations.

 

During the second phase, the participants will be creating a minimum viable product and a project for its implementation.

 

This application form is designed to help you to respond to the six criteria listed below fully and have the best possible chance at success. Please take your time and save your responses as you go through them.

 

  • Potential for impact – how does your idea relate to the challenge question? Can you explain its likely positive impact on society logically?
  • Innovation – does your idea represent a substantive new or innovative contribution in the field?
  • Use of data – how central is data to your idea?
  • Sustainability – can your idea sustain itself or be reproduced in other contexts or markets?
  • The team – is your team diverse and equipped with the skills, knowledge, and expertise they need to deliver the idea?
  • The progress your team has shown throughout the Challenge.

 

Please note that after the close of the submission period, a very short summary will appear in the community section of the Open Contracting Innovation Challenge website. This will include the name of your project, the name of your team, a very brief description (140 characters) and an image representing your idea.

Project Basics 

  1. What is the name of your project?
  2. In which country is your project based in?
  3. Which sectors are represented in your team ( civic society, government\local authorities, academia, business)
  4. Describe your project in 140 characters or less
  5. Please upload an image that represents your project or idea

 

Potential for Impact 

  1. What is the problem that you are trying to solve or improve? (150 words)
  2. What research is there to show what the problem is? What is the potential or current impact of this problem? (200 words)
  3. Who are the stakeholders, how will they benefit from your tool and how will you engage them in the project? (200 words)
  4. Who are the partners you will work with to ensure the use of your data and analysis or adoption of your tool? Do you have existing support or engagement with them? (200 words)
  5. What is your theory of change?
  6. What are the possible milestones, outputs, and outcomes of your project? How will you measure your progress?
  7. What is your capacity to deliver quantifiable outcomes or impact from your project between May – December 2021 (200 words)
  8. What risks does your project potentially face? What are the ways to mitigate these risks?

 

Innovation 

  1. What is your innovation? What is the solution, tool, or prototype you intend to deliver or build? Why is your tool innovative?
  2. How does your proposed solution solve the problem?
  3. What similar ideas exist in your countries at the moment? How is your idea different? (200 words)

 

Use of data 

  1. Is your project using, or planning to use, public contracting data or information? What specific procurement data types or fields are central to your idea? (150 words)
  2. Outline what other open data sources you are going to generate or improve, analyze or use in your prototype. Provide URLs if possible. Outline what tools you are planning to use. Was there any closed data that will be opened by your tool? (200 words)

 

Sustainability

19. What is your strategy and next steps for implementing your solution after the challenge to ensure it is widely adopted and embedded in organizations or institutional processes? (200 words)

20. What milestones have you reached so far? (Concept defined, concept user-tested, prototype built, MVP built, external users engaging with the product, impact quantified, other (please specify))

21. What will you do to make your idea sustainable beyond the Open Contracting Innovation Challenge Funding? Check out some business models. (150 words)

 

Team 

  1. How many team members do you have? (tick boxes, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, more than 5)
  2. What is the name of your first team member? (50 characters free text) (repeat these three questions for 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th depending on the answer to the previous question)
  3. What is their email address (for communication about your challenge progress) (verified email address if poss)
  4. What is their role in the team? What vital skills, knowledge, networks, and experience do they bring to the project? Which of these roles are represented in your team: public procurement expert, advocacy expert, IT-expert? (200 words)
  5. In which country/city is/are you/your team based?
  6. Is there anything else you would like to tell us about your team or project? (200 words)
